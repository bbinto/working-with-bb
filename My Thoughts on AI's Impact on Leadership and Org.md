# My thoughts on AI's Impact on Product Leadership and Organizations

March 2026

Each week, each day, AI is redefining how we work and will work, yet nobody really can predict or knows how it actually plays out. It's hard to keep up, and while no one can claim a perfect map of the future, we know that the faster the world moves, the more we must rely on our internal compass: taking a second to ask ourselves as leaders about our values and princicples is so crucial to help not only ourselves but also our team members to ground our decisions and speed on understanding that AI is a powerful accelerator, not a replacement (yet?!) for critical thinking or emotional intelligence. I believe in a philosophy of augmentation over mere generation. I'm in favour of using AI as augmentation rather than a generator so we can enhance human prodcutivity by having AI acting as a partner.

Humans remain key, and more than ever, as leaders and managers, our job is to regulary make sure we still have the right people in the right seats, and help them get better, with and without the assistance of AI.


## Guiding principles and vision

To build with uncompromising intent: Where human judgment and strategic context act as the compass for agentic execution — ensuring that our 10x increase in velocity is always directed toward the right problems.

### AI-First Principles

### 1. Direction Over Velocity
Speed is a liability without clarity, i.e. we recognize that AI can make it 10x easier to run in the wrong direction. Therefore, we prioritize "Directional Integrity": we do not start the engine until the destination is validated. Clarity of 'Why' must always outpace the speed of 'How'.

### 2. Context as the Primary Guardrail, and the Only Bottleneck
Execution is no longer the constraint; the clarity of our "Why" is. We do not celebrate the speed of shipping; we celebrate the accuracy of the direction. Before any "vibe coding" begins, the human context—the user problem and the strategic intent—must be crystal clear.

### 3. Every Member to grow as a Builder
The lines between PM, Designer, and Engineer are blurring. We will expect (with help and support) every team member to use AI-augmented tools to fill skill gaps, prototype ideas, and move from "thinking about it" to "shipping it" without waiting for central resources (e.g. PRDs can be replaced with Loveable prototypes to expedite discovery and validation). PMs must use eval techniques to validate and quarantee quality of AI-first or AI-supported features. 
    A note on execution: While "vibe coding" is super helpful to replace traditional prototyping tools and expedite discovery, it is still considered more of a "spaghetti code" approach. It is a powerful tool for validation, but I want to be clear that it cannot (yet) be used to replace "real" scalable, production-grade code.

### 4. Emotional Intelligence (EI) is as an Anchor
As AI handles the cognitive load of execution, our value lies in our humanity. We prioritize the mental health, motivation, and growth of our people. A manager’s success is measured by how much their team members improve, not just how much they produce.

### 5. Agentify, Don’t Delegate
Centralized teams (Data, Rev/Dev-Ops, User Research) should no longer be service-request bottlenecks. Their goal is to build "agentic infrastructure"—tools and protocols that allow every squad to self-serve with high-quality standards autonomously.

### 6. Serious Stewardship of Power
We respect the transformative power of the tools we use. We move fast, but we never move blindly. We approach AI development with the weight of its potential impact in mind, ensuring our innovations are ethical, secure, and truly beneficial to the user.
